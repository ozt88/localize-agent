# Esoteric Ebb

Esoteric Ebb is a narrative cRPG by Christoffer Bodegård (studio Sudden Snail) and collaborators including studios Gibbet Games and Speldosa Interactive, published by Raw Fury. Its game system is based on Dungeons & Dragons and heavily inspired by Disco Elysium and Planescape TormentC. Bodegård, r/Games post (Reddit, 14 January 2024). Esoteric Ebb - Christoffer Bodegård - A D&D Disco-like CRPG about a cleric and a goblin unraveling a political conspiracy..

The game released in March 3, 2026. The game's initial cost was $24.99 (United States dollars), €24.99, £20.99 (pounds sterling) with a -10% discount.

## Description

### Premise

The game is set in a custom D&D-based setting — in a realm called The Coast, or Realm of Jor, named after the magician who created it. It is a realm where magic works in esoteric ways (hence the title of the game) and where fantasy intersects with contemporary realism, something the developer describes as "(Post-)Arcanepunk".

It is set in an era after the Coast War, which ended the rules of human mage aristocracies (called magocrats); an era when all gods are dead; an era of rising democracies and divisive ideologies. In a great city of Norvik, district called Tolstad, a strange explosion destroyed a tea shop. The tea shop belonged to a disputed territory of goblin tribe HWBT. The district's authorities claimed that it was a normal incident, but the leader of HWBT insists on arcane investigation.

The city sends The Cleric, which the player controls. He is an arcane cleric with a peculiar connection to esoteric forces called Chimes, representations of his abilities (D&D mechanic), which narrate the game. He is joined by agent of HWBT Snell. Together they must solve the case before the first ever elections in Norvik.

### Gameplay

The game is done in a style heavily reminiscent of Disco Elysium, but used D&D 5th edition ruleset to create a narrative-heavy adventure played mostly in dialogues, where different paths through the story are gated by rolling a twenty-sided die. The player (as well as each character in the game) has six Abilities with different numerical value, which are then added to the rolled number to then arbitrate whether or not the character succeeds or fails at a task.

Additionally, the Abilities act as characters residing in Cleric's mind, giving additional information about the world and chances of success based on their numerical value. This value rises when the player accrues enough experience point to level up, or by equipping items.

The game is played in an isometric view. The Cleric moves around the world looking for interactable objects or characters, completing quests, collecting items, etc. This is reminiscent of Point & Click Adventure games, though its numbers-based gameplay over puzzle-solving makes it a computer Role-Playing Game.

Similar to Disco Elysium, the game counts in game time in minutes, where each dialogue option and action raises the time. Unlike Disco Elysium, Esoteric Ebb features a system of pseudo-combat, where the dice checks of dialogue are done in a turn-based fashion against an opponent who usually damages Cleric on their turn.

### Design philosophy

Esoteric Ebb has a focus on narrative. Like the games that influenced it, it features large amount of text and progression is done through exploration, reading dialogue, and making choices. The design of interactive story-telling is then an important part of designing Esoteric Ebb, which Christoffer Bodegård says is his interest.

His experience with D&D shaped the narrative design of Esoteric Ebb. In an interview with YouTube channel The 41st Precinct, he describes the role of a Dungeon Master (the one who facilitates the game and controls the NPC) as "wrestling with players for agency" to give players a good experience based on what they expect and what choices they are offered. For game design, agency requires player's awareness of outcomes in choices. It is important for the player to constantly get information on their choices, as well as having interesting outcomes from failures.C. Bodegård, interview with Fergus Halliday for Multiplier (21 May 2025). Is fail-forward the future for CRPGs?. Multiplier Australia. Writing for The Guardian, Danielle Lucas describes this as "the feeling of freedom in a confined space."Danielle, Lucas, (28 August 2025). Esoteric Ebb: a Dungeons & Dragons-style campaign that lets you be as stupid as you like. The Guardian. London.

In Disco Elysium, this was done through personified player statistics narrating the game and presenting choices, as well as having odds presented on each dice roll; features found in Esoteric Ebb, namely as Chimes. In the chimes we also see that "failure" (when DC for correct information isn't met) still results in getting information in a humorous manner. Some dice check failures reveal more useful information than successes, and other failures result in the same outcome as a success, albeit more laborious (and hilarious).

Additionally, Bodegård stresses the importance of minimizing "negative agency" (when the outcome is not what the player expects). This is done by informing of the likely outcome before the decision is madeC. Bodegård, post on Esoteric Ebb's Steam page (5 January 2025). An Esoteric Campaign - Writing, which in Esoteric Ebb is done by Chimes (sometimes through irony and being able to see that a Chime failed in its assessment).

In contrast to giving the player information on their agency, some behind-the-scenes information may negatively impact the player. If a choice is clearly the best and is shown because of high prerequisites, it removes agency from choosing the option. If there is indication that a choice is important, then the choices without the indicator will feel unimportant. If a choice is used to give additional information, then the player will pay less attention to the actual text.C. Bodegård, post on Esoteric Ebb's Steam page (20 September 2025). An Esoteric Campaign - Dialog Design

# A high choice-to-text ratio.
# >50% of content being dynamic.
# An open-ended design.

## Developers & Collaborators

Along with the conception of the project, Bodegård is the game's programmer, designer, and writer. Initially a solo project, the scope required many collaborators, which Bodegård calls "escargatoire" (a word for a snail nursery).

### Chris Bodegård

Between the years 2015-2018, Bodegård studied game writing and development in University of Skövde, Sweden, where he learned programming and graphical design. His bachelor's and master's theses were "on the topic of agency and UI design in CRPGs".

After graduating he worked as a freelancer, writing for Apsulov: End of Gods, Raft, and Gori: Cuddly Carnage.

### Isac Jonsson

Isac Jonsson is a freelance sound designer and audio producer from Sweden who is in charge of audio mixing, implementation, and sound design. He was the first to join the project.

In 2024, he registered a business under the name Sound RespawnRegistration information on Swedish Intermediary service allabolag.se. Allabolag.se - Sound Respawn. UC Affärsinformation AB. Stockholm., which is also name used for his social media.

Links: Instagram, YouTube.

### Oscar Westberg

Oscar Westberg is a Swedish freelance artist and comic book illustrator. His illustrations are found in the game's character and item portraits, UI icons, and promotional material.

Though his subject varies, most of his portfolio is thematically in the realm of medieval fantasy. He has made numerous comics, worked on illustrations for a novel, research company Srivatsan Lab, trading card game Seize: Guilded Treasures, and various other projects.

In April 2024 he launched a Kickstarter campaign for The Cult of Dreams: A Fantasy Journal, an illustrated, tabletop-RPG-inspired, narrative journal set in a fantasy world. It raised 80,000 Swedish Krona, 8 times more than its goal.

Links: OscarWestberg.se, Instagram, BlueSky, Tumblr, Twitter, Ko-Fi, Gumroad, INPRNT, DriveThruRPG.

### Anders Bach

Anders Bach Pedersen is a Danish musician, producer, mixing engineer, and scholar studying at the University of Copenhagen. He is one of three composers for Esoteric Ebb, whose email pitch for the game's music was almost left deleted before being re-discovered.

Bach is an author of numerous scholarly papers and articles on the subject of music and sonic aesthetics. He has composed and produced a large amount of varied music, ranging from experimental to popular. He worked with artists such as Peter Sommer, MØ, Kota Yamauchi, Svin, and the two other composers of Esoteric Ebb: Brian Batz and Kristian Paulsen.Anders Bach's website. AndersBach.

His composition is often eclectic, as he explores ambience, noise, and "destroyed melodies". He is a drummer and often works with synthesizers, but has composed for other instruments. His projects include GNOM (solo, co-produced by Brian Batz), Erna (with Kristian Paulsen) and H E X (with Lars Bech Pilgaard). He also manages the record label Wetwear.

## Trivia

* The name "Ebb" was chosen at random as a "cool name".
* Christoffer Bodegård believes he was the first to coin the term "Disco-like". Though it is a hard statement to prove, since "Disco" is also a genre of music, his usage was the first on the /r/DiscoElysium subreddit, where it now refers to other spiritual successors and aesthetics.
