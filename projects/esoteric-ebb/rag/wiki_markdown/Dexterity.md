# Dexterity

Dexterity (abbr. DEX) is a character statistic, and a character in The Cleric's mind.

It encompasses many types of skillfulness, whether it is manual labor or social wit. It is a voice of selfish greed.

## Mechanics

For more detailed information about abilities and dice rolls see Abilities.

Rolling dexterity happens more often outside of dialogue dice checks. While in dialogue checks it will be called when attempting something requiring craftsmanship, reaction time, or quick wit, it is also used exclusively for two mechanics. The first is Pickpocketing, and the second is an initiative roll, called at the start of an encounter to determine which character starts first in the exchange.

In character creation, the description lists the ability to "Pick locks & Sneak & Dodge Traps." In the demo, sneaking is done in one ability check, but not mechanically as in other stealth games. It is uncertain how the game will operate in different parts of the game, but these three actions are possibly simple dialogue dice checks.

## Personality

As a chime, Dexterity is characterized as a self-centered opportunist. It represents freedom in the way of doing anything that benefits The Cleric, and doing so without consequences. Dexterity is the love of risk for the sake of high rewards. As such, it can appear crass and enthusiastically cynical.

Dexterity insists that The Cleric is actually a "Rogue", a different Dungeons & Dragons class, though it is uncertain how diegetic the classes are in Esoteric Ebb. With high enough Dexterity, the player will find out that Cleric's nickname used to be "Dick-ass Rogue", likely referring to a time of the Crowd Pleaser and Little Weirdo Background Focus, though both are focused on Charisma.

Politically, Dexterity is the voice of the Revolutionary Freestriders, ideologically equivalent to economic liberalism, though in Dexterity often satirized as simply loving monetary gains. Dexterity defends Freestriders and overthrow of magocracies in favor of electoral system. Dexterity also seeks out profitable ventures, in contrast to Strength's moralized quests.
