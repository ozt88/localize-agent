# Visken

Visken is Tolstad's mortician and former dentist and surgeon. He is the intended first character that the player talks to, and provides useful information about the main quest, Cleric's immediate past and items, information about the city, and starts quests associated with him. Though he can be ignored or circumvented to get useful items from him, conversation with him is highly beneficial.

He can be found in Visken's Lair, the local Lichhouse (mortuary), and has permanent residence in The Holes, 16 Bogil Tunnel.

## Description

Visken is a disconcertingly pale human who always seems to be controlling himself to appear as agreeable as is necessary. His duplicitous behavior ranges from professionalism to more questionable extents.

Though he is cagey about many aspects of his life, he isn't always uncooperative when The Cleric questions him, and divulges many of his thoughts about the world when pressed. He also doesn't mind helping Cleric and even gives The Cleric the spell Charm Person and suggests practicing on Ôst. Though he doesn't mind taking most of the items found in his Visken's Lair, he reacts strongly to pickpocketing the potion of The Mind Expansion.

## Background

Being from the Reeds, he is an immigrant to Askan and Tolstad. Despite this, he praises the Party of Norvik, as (despite their chauvinistic nationalism) it benefits his economic interests and conforms to his values in regards to hierarchy and law.

He is shown to be very knowledgeable and competent, especially in magic. Many spells can be found in his lair, and he owns books ranging from folk stories to history and philosophy, including a rare copy of Norvikian Chants. Snell insinuates that he is in fact exceptionally powerful.

He bought an egg, sold to him as a basilisk egg, which he was curious to study. It was however a Kobold egg, and it spawned into Ôst, his loyal bodyguard and employee with a mindset bordering on servitude.

The quest Lichhouse Blues concerns the many ways his nature comes into suspicion. Though it can be foreshadowed with several lines and facts surrounding him without any checks, the check that confirms it and progresses the quest is a Wisdom check of DC 33, which can be lowered by witnessing the many oddities.
