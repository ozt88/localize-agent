# Strength

Strength (abbr. STR) is a character statistic, and a character in The Cleric's mind.

Similarly to its origins in Dungeons & Dragons, it represents the physical prowess and is useful in encounters and shows of strength. Unlike D&D, it represents Cleric's faith in his god Urth, as well as allegiance to the city's administration, heroism, and masculinity.

## Mechanics

For more detailed information about abilities and dice rolls see Abilities.

Strength in many ways isn't unlike other abilities. Rolls that require Strength often involve showing capability and sometimes performing it. It is an ability of force, but also of posturing. Chimes give information relating to Urth, the city of Norvik (especially its administration and Urthguard), and heroic legends. The information Strength gives is often terse and idealistic.

Unlike other abilities, higher Strength unlocks additional equipment slots for the Cleric. Since equipment increases ability score, this makes Strength one of the most useful abilities.

Combat in the demo does not feature weapon damage, so the D&D battle mechanics aren't used by the player. STR dice checks do often feature in encounters, however.

## Personality

As a chime, Strength is stern, haughty, and zealous.

Strength is the inner voice speaking for the Party Of Norvik, the law-focused status quo of the city, and as such it sometimes characterizes its ideals in a satirical fashion. Human-centered nationalism (in particular Urthfolk) and religious reverence to Urth are Strength's main interests. Strength commands respect, seeing itself as an extension of the highest authority of the Norvikian administration. It also advocates for heroic acts of exercising powers, proposes grand "quests" to save humanity, and other conventional ideals. However, it can also show a deeper understanding of the mindset of those inclined to revere power, law and tradition. Its assessment of issues plaguing society can be as nuanced as any other chime's.

The last portion of the description during character creation states: "To protect the innocent. To lead the weak. To sacrifice until nothing but your bones hit the pavement. To literally die."

In an early Steam post, Strength is defined as "search for meaning, religion, and masculinity." Strength's role as representing masculinity plays an important role, as the writer Christoffer Bodegård  says in an interview with the YouTube channel The 41st Precinct: "Esoteric Ebb is at its core about, essentially, masculinity[...]" and in its opposite femininity, which is the domain of Wisdom.
