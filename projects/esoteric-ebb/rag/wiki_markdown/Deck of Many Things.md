# Deck of Many Things

The Deck of Many Things is a canonically Legendary magic item in both Esoteric Ebb and Dungeons & Dragons 5th edition media. You can acquire the Deck of Many Things by solving the Gynosphinx's second riddle as part of the quest An Easy Riddle. The Deck can be drawn from by examining it in your inventory and choosing to draw cards. Intelligence will chime in, stating that historically this is a stupid idea, but will reiterate that if you draw a bad card, it is statistically smarter to draw only once more to minimize the losses (by hopefully outweighing whatever negative card with something overwhelmingly positive, like a boatload of cash).

It is worth noting that the order in which you receive the cards is randomized whenever a run is started, which means you cannot reload your save to get the cards in a different order.

## Effects & Examine

Equipping the Light Crossbow grants the Cleric -4 to their Wisdom.

Most Decks of Many Things have only 13 cards in them, but some can have as many as 22. In Esoteric Ebb there are only practically 6 for the purposes of gameplay and have been adjusted to better fit the balance and nature of the game (and realistically, you're not surviving more than that) but it appears to be a Deck with 22 cards since some cards like Balance and Donjon are only present in such decks.

### Flames

D&D 5e description: A powerful devil becomes your enemy. The devil seeks your ruin and plagues your life, before attempting to slay you which lasts until either you or the Devil dies.

Esoteric Ebb: In-game, Braxo, "Pencilwing" will be selected by the Deck to be the Devil to torment you. Strangely enough however, this effect does not seem to work as normally some kind of esoteric force pushes the selected devil to do its duty of tormenting you (almost as though it has ebbed, how thematic...), and Braxo offers 2 different theories. One is that he's such a bad friend that alone will be enough to cause your downfall, the second is that you are already royally screwed enough in the future and he just gets to watch it happen popcorn in hand. Either way, there appears to be no practical effect In-game and can be treated as though nothing happened.

### Balance

D&D 5e description: Your mind suffers a wrenching alteration, causing your alignment to change. If you are evil, you become good, if lawful you become chaotic and vice versa. If you are Neutral this has no effect.

Esoteric Ebb: In-game, Constitution [verify] will chime in stating that you must select one of 6 dialogue choices, each pertaining to a relevant stat in order, Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom and Charisma. Your chosen stat will then increase by 2 (an effective +1 modifier bonus), and you will be prompted to pick again but this time to decrease a stat by 2 (an effective -1 modifier penalty).

### Gem

D&D 5e description: Twenty-five pieces of jewelry worth 2,000 gp each or fifty gems worth 1,000 gp each appear at your feet.

Esoteric Ebb: In-game, you will immediately receive a large amount of "amber nickels" in your palm. Intuitively, you determine that these are worth exactly 50,000 Crowns. 50,000 Crowns are immediately put into your inventory, and you are free to spend them as you please.

### Skull

D&D 5e Description: You summon an avatar of death--a ghostly humanoid skeleton clad in a tattered black robe and carrying a spectral scythe. It appears in a space within 10 feet of you and immediately attacks you and warns your companions that you must win the fight alone.

Esoteric Ebb: In-game, Death [add real name] approaches, stating "this is my least favorite part of the job". You can choose to accept death gracefully via the underlined dialogue choice or succeed a Charisma Check [DC:??, need confirmation] to convince it to let you live by stating you are a powerful wizard and can use your power to free the Avatar of Death from its duty. Death will then jokingly say that it "has to let you go now" before offering a game of either skill or chance. After unavoidable initial PC hesitation, it will choose a game for you, Rock-Paper-Scissors, which it states to be a "Core-game". After it counts down from 3 you will be presented with 3 choices, to throw either Rock, Paper or Scissors. Choosing Rock will see you win the game, and Death will leave you be. Choosing Scissors or Paper causes you to lose and thusly die, causing a game over.

### Jester

D&D 5e description: You gain 10,000 XP, or you can draw two additional cards beyond your declared draws.

Esoteric Ebb: In-game, A winking face appears on your arm. You gain Advantage in absolutely everything you do for 72 hours (3 Days), rolling twice whenever you roll a d20 and taking the higher number.

### Donjon

D&D 5e description: You disappear and become entombed in a state of suspended animation in an extradimensional sphere. [sic] everything you had on you is left behind when you disappear and remain imprisoned until found. A Wish spell can reveal your location but cannot outright free you. Draw no more cards.

Esoteric Ebb: In-game, it's not much different. You receive an immediate game-over, receiving the unique death message "JAIL" instead of "The Strings are severed". You will have to load a previous save to continue. This also rewards you with the achievement "END OF THE STRING" if you had not already earned it for being an instant death.

### Rogue

D&D 5e description: A nonplayer character of the DM's choice becomes hostile toward you. The identity of your new enemy isn't known until the NPC or someone else reveals it. Nothing less than a Wish spell or divine intervention can end the NPC's hostility toward you.

Estoeric Ebb: During the credits at the ends of the game, it is revealed that a random old man somewhere in Norvik has become hostile towards the Cleric, plotting their demise.
