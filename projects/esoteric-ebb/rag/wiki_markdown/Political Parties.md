# Political Parties

Esoteric Ebb features several political parties that struggle for influence in the newly democratic city of Norvik.

Politics are a frequent part of the game's narration and events. The quest Election Week deals with the first elections in Norvik, which also serves as a deadline for solving the central quest The Tea Shop That Blew Up.

The main parties as presented on the Voting Card are:
* Nationalist, Party of Norvik
* Freestrider, Revolutionary Party
* Azgalist, Worker's Party
* Agrarian, Rural Party
* Arcanist, Party of Magic

Last spot is left blank for any smaller parties or an abstained vote. There are several small parties, most of which are unnamed, and which usually get subsumed by the other parties (Agrarians are said to subsume into the Azgalists). Only the Nationalists, Freestriders, and Azgalists poll higher than 5% in the polls published by The Norvikian Post (a Nationalist publication).

Small parties mentioned include Civic League of Progressives, The Torch Union, Order of the Scales, Hedonist Party, Conservational Lobby, and "various Guild parties".

Each Ability represents one of the main parties. Strength supports Nationalists, Dexterity supports Freestriders, Constitution is tied to Agrarians (although in a cryptic way, as the Ability's lines are rather esoteric), Intelligence represents the Arcanists (although it believes itself to be the only rightful autocrat), Wisdom supports Azgalists, and Charisma represents an apolitical person (as an opportunist who pretends to be on the side of everyone).

## Real world equivalents

Each party represents real political movements, some more closely than others.

Party of Norvik are the status quo ruling party. They map onto the early political right, though more specifically they have theocratic and nationalist features. Their ideology in game is referred to as numenism.

Revolutionary Freestriders are proponents of liberal democracy and capitalism.

Azgalism reflects labor movements, socialism/communism.

Arcanists propagate the return to magocracy (autocratic rule of mages) which ended 27 years prior.
