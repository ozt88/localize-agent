# Abilities

Abilities are Esoteric Ebb's primary character statistics — six attributes which represent how good a character is at given tasks, namely by modifying values of a dice roll. This system is based on Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition, although streamlined.

These are:
* Strength
* Dexterity
* Constitution
* Intelligence
* Wisdom
* Charisma

In Esoteric Ebb, abilities are also personified as Chimes.

## Ability Score & Modifier

Abilities have two numeral values associated with them.

On character creation and on leveling up, the player increases the Ability Score by one point. Score can also be increased (or decreased) by equipment. In the creator, the score's minimum is 8 and maximum is 15 (but can be higher with a Background Focus).

The second numeral value is the Ability Modifier. Modifier is a number that is added to a dice roll result, improving the chances of succeeding. It is tied directly to the Score's: Ability Score of 10 lends the Modifier 0. Every two Scores, the Modifier increases by one: Score of 12 is Modifier +1, Score of 14 is Modifier +2; it becomes a negative modifier when below 10, meaning Score of 8-9 is -1, Score of 6-7 is -2, and so on.

The player can choose two proficiencies when creating a character. The selected Abilities have a higher Modifier without increasing the Ability Score.

## Ability Check

The game's main mechanic is the Ability Check, also referred to as a dice check or dice roll. In dialogue, some choices require arbitrating whether or not the choice will succeed or fail. This is done by rolling a twenty-sided die (d20) and adding or subtracting the associated Ability Modifier.

To succeed, the rolled value needs to meet or surpass a set number called Difficulty Class (DC for short). Certain dialogue choices and circumstances can lower or raise the DC, seen when hovering over the choice.

Some failed checks can be attempted again. However, they need to be unlocked using a Shard of Jor or after Resting.

In Esoteric Ebb, Chimes have a separate DC which triggers when they chime in with information (or what they will say on visible failure). Unlike normal DC, this is not rolled and instead is passed based on Ability Score.
