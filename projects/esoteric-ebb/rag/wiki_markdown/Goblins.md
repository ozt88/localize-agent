# Goblins

]]

Goblins are a short humanoid species found in large number in Tolstad's southern quarter and underground.

## Description

Goblins are people of small stature with various skin colors and long pointy ears. They have sharp claws and fangs. Their life-span is short, a single generation being described as around 10 years.

There are 7 ethnicities in The Coast:C. Bodegård, Esoteric Ebb official Discord server. 19/11/2025 (requires Discord access to view).
* Forest goblins: Green with pointy features. Earliest seen in the game.
* Hill goblins: Yellowish with flat features (akin to most prominent depictions of Dungeons & Dragons' modern default setting Forgotten Realms.
* Cave Goblin: Grayish, smaller and bug-eyed.
* Crag Goblin: Redish, bigger and more bearded.
* Isle Goblin: Blue, more "bestial", with white manes.
* Curse Goblin: Greenish with mutated features, debated to be fey-esoterically created.
* Sand Goblin: Beige-ish, small and eyeless.

## Culture

Goblins are described by Charisma as "somewhat civilized". There are many references to portrayal of goblins by other races (especially humans) to be primitive, uncouth, and other antipathetic sentiments. Snell describes a suppression and even oppression of goblins in Norvik, supported by historical accounts given throughout the game.

Their society is heavily communal, described as "tribal". Goblin children grow up in nurseries with the entire adult community raising them. In Askan there are three goblin tribes, the most influential (though small) being the Halfway Bridge Tribe found in Tolstad, which plays a central role in the main quest The Tea Shop that Blew Up.

The three tribes of Askan are:
* The Halfway Bridge Tribe: Forest goblins led by Miska Sageleaf in Tolstad.
* The Ishkii Tribe: Cave goblins referred to as water goblins for their river expertise, led by a council of three Nilbogs in the lighthouse of Dvârgby.
* NikMullach's Below Tribe: Hill goblins led by the NikMullach family, who run the most active adventuring guild in Norvik, residing below the Housing District.

Their language (though Askanii goblins do not speak it) in the game is Scottish Gaelic.

The ethnicity most commonly seen in Tolstad is called "Forest goblins". A number of goblins of other ethnicities can also be encountered, such as Razz (a Hill goblin), and Miss Nearly (a Cave goblin).

### Goblin Genocide

Towards the end of the Arcane Era (exactly fifty years prior to the events of the game) Urth destroyed the many tribes of goblins in Askan. Three tribes were selected to undergo an "integration program", where children were schooled by humans, resulting in goblin language and culture being erased.

This mirrors forced assimilation seen in the real world, such as the boarding schools of United States and Canada. Goblin language being Gaelic may also be in reference to Anglicisation in the British Isles.

## Trivia

* When Ettir speaks Goblin, she says "Ta mi. Nach ta thu, Gobhar?" which the game translates as "I do indeed. Don’t you, goblin?" where the native name for goblins is Gobhar. In Scottish Gaelic, gobhar IPA /ˈɡ̊o.əɾ/ means "goat". Though having some surface resemblance to the word "goblin", the "bh" is silent.
* In Dungeons & Dragons, goblins have been a staple enemy monster since the beginning of the game, appearing even in the precursor game Chainmail. Though their depiction varies, the current official setting of D&D Forgotten Realms portrays them as having yellow to red (rarely green) skin and broad noses. This contrasts to the Forest goblins of Esoteric Ebb, following a modern convention of the mythological creature, such as in Spider-Man and Warhammer.
